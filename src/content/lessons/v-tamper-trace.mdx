Red team vs. blue team: tamper with the hardware, then switch sides and trace the evidence.

Red tries to run a prohibited training run while making the hardware-verification stack keep reporting “compliant.” Blue spends a limited budget on detection layers. The lesson lives in the gap: every tamper defeats one layer but leaves a residual tell that an independent layer can catch. No single mechanism holds — verification is Swiss cheese.
