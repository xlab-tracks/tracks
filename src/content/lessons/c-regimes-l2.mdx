## Running the model

You now have the whole regime model, and everything the analysis takes is already in your hands from the previous lesson and readings: read the three states, price each technique's basket against the regime's price table, and ask which channel — usefulness, evidence, direct — its payoff flows through. What the prices and channels say, the portfolio should reflect.

Every portfolio answer in this module takes a three-part shape:

1. **The allocation** — how the effort splits across the technique menu.
2. **Where the marginal slice of effort goes, and through which channel.**
3. **Which spend binds** — the aisle the team would trade any other for.

## Exercise — choose the portfolio

The exercise stipulates a regime. Alongside the three states, each regime names the capability stage it is approaching — in the [AI Futures Model](https://blog.aifutures.org/p/ai-futures-model-dec-2025-update)'s milestones (Automated Coder, Superhuman AI Researcher, Superintelligent AI Researcher) — and the initial plan it starts under, in the sense of [Plans A, B, C, and D for misalignment risk](https://blog.redwoodresearch.org/p/plans-a-b-c-and-d-for-misalignment): roughly, A = a strong international agreement buys a long slowdown, B = the government treats buying lead time as a national-security priority, C = the leading lab spends its few-months lead on safety without government help, D = leadership isn't taking the risk seriously and a small safety team works with what it has.

Price the baskets against the regime's table, walk the menu, and divide the effort — then defend the result in the three-part template.
